ECN, PRO or PRIME? Choosing the right account for your style

All three PRIME DCX accounts open from the same $100 deposit, trade the same 150+ markets and run on the same execution stack. What differs is how you pay for liquidity and how much leverage you can reach for. Pick by style, not by name.

The three doors

  • ECN — from 0.3 pips, $7/lot commission, 1:100. Raw aggregated pricing with the cost broken out explicitly. No dealing desk, fastest routing, scalping expressly allowed.
  • PRO — from 1.5 pips, zero commission, 1:200. One clean number per trade. Comes with a personal manager and every platform supported.
  • PRIME — from 0.4 pips, zero commission, 1:300. Near-raw spreads without a commission line, the platform's highest leverage, priority support and VIP conditions.

Decide by frequency

If you trade many times a day — scalps, quick momentum bursts — cost per round turn dominates everything. ECN's raw pricing usually wins that arithmetic, and the 60-second minimum holding window in the platform terms is the rule to design around.

If you trade a few times a week — swing entries held for days — a 1-pip difference is noise against a 100-pip target. PRO's simplicity is worth more than the spread it costs.

If you size up with conviction and want margin headroom, PRIME's 1:300 with 0.4-pip pricing is built for exactly that — with the caveat that headroom is not an obligation to use it.

Same guardrails everywhere: margin call at 100%, stop-out at 30%, minimum trade 0.01 lots, maximum 100 lots. The risk framework doesn't change between accounts — only the pricing does.

The honest answer

Most traders should start on PRO, learn what their real frequency and size look like over a month, then move to ECN (if the commission math wins) or PRIME (if leverage and near-raw pricing matter more). Switching is an email, not a migration.
