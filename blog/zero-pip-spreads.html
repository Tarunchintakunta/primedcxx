What 0.0-pip spreads actually mean — and when you'll really see them

“Spreads from 0.0 pips” is probably the most quoted line in retail trading. It is also the most misunderstood. The sentence is true — and still, if you expect every EUR/USD trade to cost you nothing, you will be disappointed. This piece explains what raw pricing actually is, where the zero prints come from, and how to think about your true cost per trade.

Where a spread comes from

Every tradable price you see is really two prices: the best bid anyone will pay and the best ask anyone will sell at. The spread is simply the distance between them. On an ECN-style feed, those two prices come from competing liquidity providers streaming quotes into one aggregated book. Nobody “sets” the spread — it emerges from competition.

When the book is deep and calm — say, EUR/USD during the London–New York overlap — the best bid and best ask routinely land on the same price. That is a genuine 0.0-pip moment. When the book is thin — Sunday open, a red-flag news release, exotic pairs — the distance widens. Both states are the same honest mechanism.

Raw spread ≠ free trading

On a raw account, the broker adds nothing to the spread and charges an explicit commission instead. On PRIME DCX, the ECN account prices from 0.3 pips with a $7 round-turn commission per lot, while the PRO account folds cost into the spread itself (from 1.5 pips, zero commission) and the PRIME account sits between (from 0.4 pips, zero commission).

The only number that matters is all-in cost: spread cost + commission, measured in dollars per lot. A 0.1-pip spread with $7 commission ($1 ≈ 0.1 pip on a standard EUR/USD lot) is an all-in ~0.8 pips. A 1.5-pip spread with no commission is an all-in 1.5 pips. Do this arithmetic once and the account choice becomes obvious for your style.

When you'll actually see 0.0

  • Session overlaps. London–New York (13:30–16:30 GMT) is the deepest window in FX; majors print their tightest spreads here.
  • Majors, not exotics. EUR/USD, USD/JPY and GBP/USD carry the most competing quotes. An exotic cross may never print zero.
  • Quiet, not dead. Deep books need activity without panic. Pre-news lulls often widen spreads as providers pull quotes.

How to verify it yourself

Open the Web Trader, pin EUR/USD to your watchlist, and watch the bid–ask distance through a London morning. Measuring your own fills — entry price versus quoted price, plus commission — for twenty trades tells you more than any marketing page, including this one.
